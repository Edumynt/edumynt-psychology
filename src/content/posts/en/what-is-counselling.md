# What Is Counselling? Purpose and Process

## Quick answer

Counselling is a professional helping process where a trained person supports someone in understanding problems and making healthy decisions.

If you remember only one line for an exam, remember this: **What Is Counselling? Purpose and Process is about rapport, confidentiality, guidance distinction.**

## Simple explanation

Think of psychology like a friendly detective for human behaviour. It does not only ask “what happened?” It asks “why did it happen, how can we observe it, and what evidence supports the answer?”

A student confused about career choices talks with a counsellor to explore interests, strengths, and options.

For beginners, the safest method is to connect every term with three things:

1. the definition,
2. one daily-life example, and
3. one exam keyword.

## Real-life example

Imagine an exam student preparing for a competitive test. The student reads a concept, tries practice questions, gets feedback, and changes the next study plan. This small situation can show attention, memory, motivation, emotion, and learning at the same time. The topic of **What Is Counselling** helps you name the exact process inside that situation.

## Exam angle

Exams usually test this topic in simple but tricky ways. Focus on:

- exact meaning and keywords: rapport, confidentiality, guidance distinction;
- difference from nearby terms;
- one classic psychologist or theory if connected;
- one practical classroom or daily-life example;
- MCQs that change only one word in the option.

A good answer should be short, clear, and example-based. Avoid writing a story when the question asks for a definition.

## Common mistakes and confusions

- **Memorising without examples:** definitions become weak when you cannot attach a real case.
- **Mixing similar terms:** many psychology terms sound close, but exams expect a clean difference.
- **Using moral judgement instead of psychology:** psychology explains behaviour with evidence; it does not simply call people good or bad.
- **Ignoring context:** the same behaviour can have different meanings in different situations.

## Summary

Counselling is a professional helping process where a trained person supports someone in understanding problems and making healthy decisions. In exam preparation, learn the definition first, then attach one simple example, then compare it with related terms. This makes the concept easy to recall and safer for MCQs.
