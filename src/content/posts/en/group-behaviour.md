# Group Behaviour: Crowd and Group Difference

## Quick answer

Group behaviour is how people act when they are part of a group, where norms, roles, leadership, and pressure affect behaviour.

If you remember only one line for an exam, remember this: **Group Behaviour is about group norms, crowd, roles.**

## Simple explanation

Think of psychology like a friendly detective for human behaviour. It does not only ask “what happened?” It asks “why did it happen, how can we observe it, and what evidence supports the answer?”

A study group may make a quiet student participate more, while a crowd may make people act more emotionally.

For beginners, the safest method is to connect every term with three things:

1. the definition,
2. one daily-life example, and
3. one exam keyword.

## Real-life example

Imagine an exam student preparing for a competitive test. The student reads a concept, tries practice questions, gets feedback, and changes the next study plan. This small situation can show attention, memory, motivation, emotion, and learning at the same time. The topic of **Group Behaviour: Crowd and Group Difference** helps you name the exact process inside that situation.

## Common mistakes and confusions

- **Memorising without examples:** definitions become weak when you cannot attach a real case.
- **Mixing similar terms:** many psychology terms sound close, but exams expect a clean difference.
- **Using moral judgement instead of psychology:** psychology explains behaviour with evidence; it does not simply call people good or bad.
- **Ignoring context:** the same behaviour can have different meanings in different situations.
